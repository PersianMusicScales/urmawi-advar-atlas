# Scholarly and Editorial Notes

## Verified in this build
1. All eighteen stored sounding-length fractions are reduced.
2. Frequency ratios are exact reciprocals of sounding lengths.
3. The ordered lattice rises strictly from 1/1 to 2/1.
4. The adjacent pattern contains twelve limmas and five Pythagorean commas.
5. The product of those adjacent steps closes the octave.
6. The exact T–T–B tetrachord identity is 4/3.
7. Bridge and nut coordinates are shared by the monochord and illustrative instrument overlays.

## Deliberately qualified
- The sequential construction narrative is a modern pedagogical reconstruction.
- The conventional 3-limit table is an analytical model, not a claim that all medieval practical intonation was fixed to one set of cent values.
- The four-string instrument is an illustrative mapping, not a reconstruction of a specific surviving instrument.
- The multilingual terminology requires specialist native-speaker and philological review before formal scholarly publication.
